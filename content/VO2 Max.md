VO2 Max is the highest rate at which a person can use oxygen during strenuous exercise. It is an indicator of aerobic fitness and endurance performance. [^1]

Achieving VO2 Max can be done by working at maximum intensity for four minutes, followed by a four-minute rest period. Repeat this cycle four times weekly and measure your VO2 Max annually to measure progress. Apple Health and other fitness trackers have built-in VO2 Max measurements, though they may be less accurate than tests at a medical facility.

Having a VO2 Max below average doubles the risk of death, even more significant than the risk posed by smoking.

If you are new to exercising, focus on [[Zone 2 Training]] for 6 months before starting VO2 Max Training.
